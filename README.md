# Evaluation of a Laser Anemometer for Turbulence Measurements (1974)

This repository shares and highlights the 1974 PhD thesis **Evaluation of a Laser Anemometer for Turbulence Measurements**.

## Author
J. David Roberts

## Year
1974

## Published copy
Zenodo: https://zenodo.org/records/19320502

## What this thesis is about
This thesis evaluates the use of a laser anemometer for measuring turbulence without inserting a probe into the flow. At the time, this was an important step toward non-intrusive turbulence measurement.

The work studies how laser-based velocity measurements can be used to estimate turbulent flow properties, including velocity fluctuations and aspects of the turbulence energy spectrum. It also examines important measurement effects such as probe-volume averaging, optical configuration, and signal interpretation.

## Why it still matters
This thesis sits near the early transition from intrusive probe measurements toward modern optical flow diagnostics such as laser Doppler velocimetry (LDV) and particle image velocimetry (PIV). It is relevant to the history of experimental fluid mechanics, turbulence measurement, and laser diagnostics.
